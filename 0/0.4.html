 Definitions to Remember

 Evaporation ()

The process by which a liquid changes into a gas (vapor) when heated. The heat energy makes the liquid molecules move faster and escape into the air.

Example: Water boiling in a beaker over a burner and turning into steam.

 Condensation ( /    )

The process by which a gas (vapor) changes back into a liquid when cooled. When the hot gas hits a cold surface, it loses its heat energy, slows down, and groups together to form water droplets.

Example: Hot steam hitting a cold metal plate and turning into liquid water drops.
