Title: Properties of air

Objective: To prove that oxygen is present in air and supports burning.

Required materials: Matchbox/lighter, candle, glass tumbler, bowl, water.

Procedure: A burning candle was kept in a bowl partially filled with water. The candle was covered with a glass tumbler.

Observation: The candle extinguished after a moment. The water volume raised in the glass tumbler.

Result: Oxygen in the air supports burning. When consumed, water takes its place (filling about 21% of the glass).

Conclusion: Oxygen is present in the air and takes up roughly 21% of its volume.
